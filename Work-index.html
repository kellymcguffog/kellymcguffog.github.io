Illustrations

Contrast

Charcoal on Paper

This was my final project in Drawing 1. It was based off of a picture that I took of my friend, Amanda, for my photography class. She is posed with one hand in the air and the other hand by her side. Her head is tilted over her shoulder and is facing her side. The top portion of her body is nude with painted straight black lines running down her back. The straightness of the lines on her back juxtapose the curvature of her body. This effect is intensified by the dramatic lighting casted on her back.

Portrait of Female

Charcoal on Paper

This portrait of a female model was completed in Drawing1. It is a rendered drawing in black and white charcoal. The dramatic lighting in this piece highlights the wrinkles in the woman’s skin and the folds of her shirt. The emotion in her face suggests that she is tired and overworked.

Still Life of Apples

Pastels on Paper

This is a still life drawing of a sliced apple and a pomegranite resting on a red tablecoth. It was created in Drawing 2 using only red, white, and black pastels. The different shades of red in the objects are intensified by the dramatic lighting beaming down on them. It draws the viewer’s eyes to the smooth skin of the fruit and compliments its richness.

The 1920s

Adobe Programming

This illustration was designed in Pictures for Communication through the use of Illustrator and Photoshop. It is a single paneled narrative of a man sitting at a bar staring eeringly at a flapper dancing. The limited detail in the background forces the viewer to use context clues to understand the narrative. For example, the woman’s costume suggests that she is a flapper, and therefore, it is the 1920s during the prohibition.

Embarrassing Story

Subheading

This illustration was designed in Digital Design with the use of Illustrator and Photoshop. It is a single paged narrative with nine different panels. It tells my most embarrassing story of the time that I thought that everybody was looking at me at the beach because of how good I looked that day, when in reality I had a pair of my mother's underwear stuck to the back of my shirt.
